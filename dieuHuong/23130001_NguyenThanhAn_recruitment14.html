1. Definition of Lifelong Learning

There is no exact definition of Lifelong Learning, but in general, it is the process where individuals continuously learn, develop knowledge, enhance skills, and advance their careers throughout their lives. It is a proactive process that does not depend on location, meaning learning can happen anywhere, such as in school, through online courses, attending workshops, reading books, or gaining practical work experience.

Additionally, lifelong learning is not limited by age, career stage, or circumstances, meaning no matter your age or career level, if you want to improve yourself, you can always learn.

2. The Importance of Lifelong Learning

In a competitive job market, continuous learning is the key to establishing your value. By exploring, learning, and developing new skills and knowledge, you expand your thinking and encounter new ideas and perspectives from those around you. This helps to foster innovation and creativity in your work and reduces biases based on outdated experiences.

In practice, people with lifelong learning abilities tend to have better adaptability and resilience when facing change. They are willing to step out of their comfort zone to explore new approaches and methods.

In the era of AI and an overload of information, exploring and learning more knowledge helps you develop deep critical thinking. Rather than just receiving and following existing information, you will continually ask questions and analyze issues from multiple perspectives. This not only enables you to solve problems effectively but also sharpens key skills such as judgment and decision-making.

3. How to Maintain Lifelong Learning

So how can you build and maintain continuous learning skills when you spend 8 hours a day at work, not to mention overtime, and after work, you just want to relax by scrolling through social media to unwind after a tiring day?

The key is to maintain curiosity. Nurture your curiosity and desire for knowledge. Instead of simply accepting things as they are, always ask why, to dig deeper into the essence of things. Keep exploring and learning new topics, even those outside your field of expertise.

Additionally, set specific goals for yourself (short-term goals for 1 year, long-term goals for 3 years or more) and make a plan to achieve those goals. Setting goals helps you focus and provides motivation for learning to reach them.
