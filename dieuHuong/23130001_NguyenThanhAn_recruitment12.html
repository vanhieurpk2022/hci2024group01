Philosophy of "Right People" According to Jim Collins

The idea of "right people" has been deeply discussed by author Jim Collins in his book "Good to Great". I would like to quote a few excerpts from the book to better understand this philosophy and how we are applying it in our recruitment process to find the right people:

"In evaluating 'right people,' companies that make the leap focus more on personal traits than academic qualifications, practical skills, specialized knowledge, or work experience. This doesn’t mean that expertise or skills are unimportant, but they are seen as things that can be learned, while traits such as personality, work ethic, reasoning ability, determination to achieve goals, and other core values are deeply embedded in a person."

According to this viewpoint, knowledge and skills can be learned and improved, but personality, work attitude, and core values are the fundamental and difficult-to-change elements that make a difference.

Applying the "Right People" Philosophy at BookingCare

At BookingCare, we have been and are applying the "right people" philosophy in our recruitment process. In addition to qualifications and experience, we place great emphasis on understanding the personality, values, and cultural fit of candidates.

In interviews, we often ask open-ended questions to better understand the candidate’s personality, what motivates them, the values they pursue, and how they behave in various situations. From there, we identify the candidate who is the best fit for the position. We value the "right people first" approach to building the team and growing together.

A typical example is when we hired for the Content Marketing position, where we sought a candidate who not only had the right professional skills but also the ability to work independently, creative thinking, a high sense of responsibility, and a strong self-learning spirit. In the end, we found someone who matched these criteria, and to this day, they have been with the company for 3 years and become a core member of the team.

We believe that investing in searching for and recruiting the right people will help place the right individuals in the right roles on the company’s journey. Once we have the right people, in the right positions, and doing the right work, we will together shape the direction and steer the company toward greater goals.

Asking "who" before "what" is considered a guiding principle throughout the development process at BookingCare. By investing in the right people, we will create a civilized working environment where everyone grows together.
